# FAQ & Objection Handling

**Owner:** COO
**Updated:** 2026-03-13
**Audience:** For COO use during outreach conversations; not customer-facing (yet)

---

## Common Objections

### "We already have a POS — Square handles ordering fine."

**Response:**
> Square is great for in-store transactions, but it wasn't designed as a customer-facing ordering experience. Drink-UX sits on top of Square — it doesn't replace it. Your staff still rings out orders in Square the same way. Drink-UX just gives customers a much better way to *place* their order and customize it without pointing at a screen or verbally explaining it to staff. Think of it as Square's front door, not a replacement.

**Key point:** This is additive, not a replacement. No retraining needed.

---

### "We're not really set up for mobile ordering — we're counter service."

**Response:**
> Drink-UX works great as a counter-side experience too — customers can customize their drink on a shared tablet or their own phone while waiting in line. It also works for ahead-of-time ordering before they arrive. You don't have to change your whole flow — you just give customers an option that removes the verbal back-and-forth on "oat milk, two pumps, extra shot."

**Key point:** Flexible deployment — not just remote/advance ordering.

---

### "It's too expensive — we're a small shop."

**Response:**
> At $49/month founding rate, that's about $1.60/day. If it saves your staff 2–3 minutes per complex order and you're seeing 30+ customized drinks a day, it pays for itself in staff time alone. And this is the founding rate — it locks in for as long as you're a customer. Standard rate after launch will be $79/month.

**Key point:** ROI framing + urgency on founding rate.

---

### "I don't want to change what's already working."

**Response:**
> Totally fair — and you don't have to change anything about your current process. We set it up alongside what you're already doing. If it's not helping within 30 days, there's nothing to cancel — the trial is free. The risk here is really just 30 minutes of your time to set it up.

**Key point:** Low-risk trial, zero disruption framing.

---

### "Our customers aren't tech-savvy — they won't use an app."

**Response:**
> Drink-UX isn't an app — there's nothing to download. It's just a web page you can access from any phone browser. We can put a QR code at the counter or on your menu. If customers don't want to use it, nothing changes. But the ones who do will love it — especially for complex orders.

**Key point:** No download, no app store friction.

---

### "We have seasonal menus — this would be hard to maintain."

**Response:**
> Seasonal updates are really easy — Drink-UX pulls from your Square catalog, so when you update items in Square, the ordering experience updates automatically. You can also manually hide/show items from the Drink-UX admin panel in about 30 seconds. Seasonal menus are actually one of the things it handles best.

**Key point:** Square-native sync means low maintenance overhead.

---

### "What if it goes down or something breaks?"

**Response:**
> We offer same-business-day support for founding customers — direct email access to the team. If the ordering experience ever has an issue, customers fall back to your normal counter process — nothing breaks your ability to take orders. It's additive, so there's no single point of failure.

**Key point:** Redundancy by design; founding customer SLA.

---

### "We already tried a different ordering system and it was a mess."

**Response:**
> That's really common — a lot of ordering solutions require hardware changes, staff retraining, or they duplicate your POS setup and create reconciliation headaches. Drink-UX is Square-native from day one, so it reads directly from your existing catalog. We've specifically built it so there's no double-entry or separate system to maintain. Happy to show you exactly where previous setups usually cause problems and how we've addressed them.

**Key point:** Differentiate from clunky ordering platforms; Square-native advantage.

**Response:**

I don't know exactly when that is, but it's filling faster than expected. Happy to send you a quick recap email you can share with your partner, or schedule a call with both of you together.

**Key point:** Acknowledge it, create soft urgency, offer a path forward.

### "Can you just set it up for us? We don't have time to manage new software."

**Response:**

After that, it's pretty much maintenance-free unless you're adding new items. I'll also check in at day 7 and day 30 to make sure everything is running smoothly.

**Key point:** White-glove onboarding is the COO doing the heavy lifting.
